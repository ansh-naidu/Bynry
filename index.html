Who we call first, and why.

Having 162 partners means nothing without a clear framework for sequencing outreach. Every hour spent on a Tier-3 partner is an hour not spent closing a Tier-1.

Tier 1 — 18 Partners

Active utility influence, multi-state reach, documented RFP involvement in 2022–2024. Founder involvement justified. Targeted for signed agreement within 90 days.

Tier 2 — 47 Partners

Utility adjacent — implementation or compliance work. Limited direct RFP influence but strong relationship potential. Nurture cadence; re-evaluate at 6 months.

Tier 3 — 97 Partners

General IT/cloud or minimal utility depth. Monitor for sector pivot signals. Only engage if warm referral surfaces or they attend utility-specific event.

Why Partners Were Deprioritized

  • ● Big-4 consulting arms (Deloitte, Accenture): Tier 3. Deal cycles 18+ months; Bynry is below their minimum deal threshold. Not a fit today.
  • ● Generic SBE/MBE IT firms: Tier 3 unless confirmed utility work. SBE status alone is not a qualifier — utility depth is.
  • ● Telecom-focused integrators: Excluded. AMI/metering infrastructure experience ≠ billing/CIS knowledge. Different buyer, different motion.
  • ● Partners already committed exclusively to Oracle/Sensus: Tier 3. Conflict of interest; engagement costs political capital without revenue upside.

Task 03 — Partner Engagement & Trust-Building

How you earn the right to pitch.

The fastest way to kill a partnership is to lead with the pitch. Partners need to believe Bynry will make them look good with their clients before they'll put their reputation on the line.

Cold Outreach Logic

Used when no warm path exists. Lead with their problem, not Bynry's product. Specifically: "I noticed you advised on the [X] utility's billing modernization in 2023 — we're seeing the same procurement pattern across 15+ utilities in [state]. Would 20 minutes be useful to share what we're seeing?"

The goal of first touch is not to pitch. It's to get a conversation where you can diagnose their current GTM motion.

Warm Outreach Logic

When introduced by an existing partner or contact, the message leads with the mutual connection and then shifts to a specific, concrete joint opportunity: "Sarah from [Partner A] suggested we connect — she thought we might be working with some of the same water utilities in the Southeast."

Warm paths convert 3–5x better. Every conversation should end with "who else should I be talking to?"

Step-by-Step Engagement Flow

Step 1 — First Touch
Research-backed, personalized outreach
Reference a specific piece of their work (RFP they advised, conference talk, utility they worked with). One short ask: 20-minute call. Never mention "partnership" in first message — it triggers a procurement/legal delay.
Step 2 — Discovery Call
Understand their utility GTM before presenting anything
Ask: How do you win utility deals? Where does CIS/billing come up in your engagements? What platforms do your clients currently use? What's missing? Listen for 80% of the call. Only share Bynry context when they ask or when it's directly relevant to what they've shared.
Step 3 — Credibility Building
Send value before asking for anything
Within 48 hours of discovery call: send a short 2-page summary of what you heard + one resource that addresses their stated gap (could be a Bynry case study, a utility RFP template, or a market insight). No ask. Just value. This is what separates Bynry from the 10 other vendors who emailed them this week.
Step 4 — Joint Opportunity Identification
Find the first real deal together
On the second or third call, ask: "Are you currently working with any utilities who are evaluating their billing or CIS platform?" If yes — this becomes the pilot account. Structure a specific joint go-to-market for that one account before formalizing any broader agreement.
Step 5 — Pilot Agreement
Sign simple, fast, one-page agreement
Start with a one-page referral or co-selling MOU for the pilot account. Not a full partner agreement. Lower legal friction = faster activation. Full agreement follows after first deal closes.
Step 6 — First Win + Relationship Deepening
Make the partner look exceptional to their client
Bynry's implementation team delivers. Partner takes credit with their client. Formal partnership agreement signed. Begin planning second account together. Share outcome metrics with partner to reinforce the value they provided.

Risks and Failure Points

High
Partner recommends Bynry but utility buys legacy vendor

Mitigation: Give partners a competitive displacement narrative they can use with utility CFOs — total cost of ownership analysis showing 40–60% lower TCO vs Oracle/Sensus over 5 years. Make it easy for them to defend the recommendation.

High
Partner signs agreement but never refers a deal

Mitigation: No "strategic partnership" agreements without a named target account in the contract. The agreement includes a 90-day activation clause — if no joint opportunity in 90 days, partnership downgrades to "affiliate" status.

Medium
Integration partner creates dependency / lock-in risk

Mitigation: Integration agreements are non-exclusive. Bynry maintains API compatibility with multiple AMI/payment vendors. Never let one integration partner become a de-facto gatekeeper to Bynry's customer base.

Low
Cloud partner co-sell doesn't convert to real pipeline

Mitigation: Cloud co-sell is primarily a credibility and marketplace play at this stage. Revenue expectations are managed accordingly. Cloud partners earn their place in the mix through funded POC support and co-marketing, not direct referrals.

Task 05 — Founder Meeting Setup & Preparation

Founder time is non-renewable. Protect it.

The Founder joining a partner meeting signals Bynry's commitment at the highest level. That signal loses value if it's used casually. Strict qualification gates ensure every Founder meeting moves the partnership forward meaningfully.

Qualification Gate — When Founder Joins

  • Partner is Tier 1 (qualified by the framework in Task 2)
  • At least one discovery call completed; clear opportunity scope defined
  • Named target utility account identified (or senior stakeholder confirmed at partner side)
  • Partner contact is decision-maker or directly reports to one
  • Previous touchpoint showed genuine interest, not just courtesy
  • Meeting has a specific agenda, not a generic "let's explore"

Scheduling Protocol

  • Propose 3 specific time slots (not "when are you free?")
  • Confirm 24 hours in advance with a calendar hold + agenda
  • Brief sent to Founder 24 hours before meeting (format below)
  • 30-minute call cap; extend only on Founder's signal
  • Post-meeting debrief note sent to partner within 2 hours
  • Next steps documented in CRM same day

Task 06 — Objections, Stalled Partners & Activation

Partners stall. Plans don't survive contact. Here's the playbook.

Partner behavior is rarely malicious — it's almost always bandwidth and priority. The job is to make Bynry the easiest, most risk-free partnership on their plate.

Objection-Handling Framework

"We don't know Bynry's track record."
Response: "Fair — let me connect you with the VP Operations at [existing utility customer] who went live in 11 weeks. She was skeptical too. 15-minute reference call?" Proof over pitch, always.
"We already work with [Oracle/Neptune/Sensus]."
Response: "Makes sense — those are the right choice for large utilities. We're not competing with them. Our customers are the 3,000–50,000 connection utilities your larger clients refer down to. This is additive to your portfolio, not a conflict."
"Let's revisit in Q3."
Response: "Totally understand. Before we pause — is there a specific concern I could address today that would make Q3 more valuable? If not, I'll send one relevant resource monthly and reconnect on [specific date]." Set a calendar event; don't disappear.
"We're not sure how the commercials would work."
Response: "Let me send you a one-page structure our other partners have used — most found it simpler than expected. If it feels off, we can shape it together." Reduce the mental load; don't ask them to invent the deal structure.
"We don't have capacity right now."
Response: "Understood. What if we started smaller — just flag Bynry when a client mentions billing pain, and I do the rest? No commitment on your end until we prove value." Lower the bar to zero.

Follow-Up Cadence — Stalled Partners

Week 1

Post-meeting debrief note sent. Thank them + document agreed next steps. Share one concrete piece of value.

Week 3

If no response: send a relevant trigger (utility news, a client story, a short market insight) — no ask. Just staying visible and useful.

Week 6

If still silent: direct message. "I want to respect your time. Should I close this out, or is there a better time to reconnect? Either answer works." This usually prompts a reply.

Activation Strategy — Signed but Inactive Partners

A signed partner who hasn't referred a deal within 60 days is a paperwork exercise, not a revenue asset. Activation is a distinct motion from signing.

  • Anchor to a specific account: "We have a warm lead at [Utility X] in your market. Can we work this one together?" Give them a reason to activate now, not in theory.
  • Remove friction from their side: Provide a 1-page brief they can forward to their client. Don't ask them to translate or explain Bynry.
  • Create a shared win target: "Let's both aim to present SMART360 to two utilities together by end of quarter — that gives us a real basis to evaluate the partnership." Shared accountability.
  • Executive escalation if stalled at staff level: If the day-to-day contact isn't activating, request a 15-minute call with their VP or partner-level leader. Frame it as "wanting to make sure we're supporting you at the right level."
  • Downgrade if truly inactive: After 90 days of no activity post-signing, reclassify to Tier 3. Preserve the relationship, reduce investment of time. Don't report it as an active partnership to the Founder.
